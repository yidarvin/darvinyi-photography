# Photo-example directives

One entry per chapter. When building a chapter, read its entry and source the real,
licensed photos it calls for, following `public/photos/README.md`. These are teaching
photographs that sit alongside the chapter's one inline-SVG signature figure, not
replacements for it. Prefer the author's own Leica Q3 / Hasselblad X2D II frames;
otherwise use licensed work (Unsplash, Pexels, Wikimedia Commons) with attribution.

Skip a photo only when a diagram genuinely teaches the idea better (many Part I and
Part III mechanism chapters are diagram-first). Composition, light, color, and
black-and-white chapters should lean on real photographs.

## Part I --- How a camera works

- **the-exposure-triangle** - optional: three frames of one scene at matched exposure but different settings (wide aperture / slow shutter / high ISO look). Diagram-first chapter; a photo triptych is a bonus, not required.
- **aperture-and-depth-of-field** - two portraits at the same framing, one shallow (wide aperture, background dissolved) and one deep (stopped down). If possible, the same subject on the Q3 (full frame) and X2D (medium format) at the same f-number to show the format difference.
- **shutter-speed-and-motion** - a motion pair: one frozen (fast shutter, sharp) and one intentional blur (slow shutter, streaked water / lights / a walking figure).
- **iso-sensor-and-dynamic-range** - a high-ISO low-light frame showing grain, and a base-ISO high-contrast scene with detail held from shadow to highlight.
- **focus-and-autofocus** - a shallow-DoF frame with the focus plane clearly on the eyes; optionally a focus-and-recompose miss to show the plane shift.
- **metering-and-exposure-decisions** - a backlit or snowy/high-key scene the meter would misjudge, exposed the photographer's way; pair with its histogram if available.
- **how-a-shutter-uncovers-the-frame** - diagram-first; no photo required.
- **rolling-shutter-and-readout** - a real rolling-shutter artifact: a skewed fast-moving object (car, propeller, panned pole) or LED banding, shot with an electronic shutter.
- **flash-sync-and-the-leaf-advantage** - a daylight fill-flash / overpowered-sun portrait only a leaf shutter can make (subject lit, sky held down, wide aperture). Q3 or X2D if possible.
- **lenses-optics-and-perspective** - a compression pair: the same subject shot wide-and-close vs long-and-far, showing perspective change; optionally a diffraction-softened stopped-all-the-way-down frame.
- **choosing-a-lens** - a small set of the same scene at 28 / 35 / 50 / 90 (Q3 crops or XCD focal lengths) showing how focal length reframes the story.

## Part II --- Shooting aesthetically

- **reading-light** - a set showing light quality: front vs side vs back light on a face or object, and hard vs soft (small hard source vs overcast/diffused). This chapter is photo-heavy.
- **composition-fundamentals** - clear real examples of each device: rule of thirds, leading lines, a frame-within-a-frame, and negative space. One strong photo per device.
- **advanced-composition** - layered depth (foreground/mid/background), gestalt grouping, and dynamic tension. Photos where the structure is obvious once pointed out.
- **color-science-for-photographers** - frames built on color relationships: a complementary pair (teal/orange), an analogous scene, and a near-monochrome color field. Real photos, not swatches.
- **black-and-white-seeing** - the same scene imagined in color and in mono to show tonal separation; a red-filter-style sky (dark sky, bright clouds). Strong tonal-contrast frames.

## Part III --- Editing

- **the-digital-negative** - a flat, unprocessed raw next to its finished version, to show latitude. Diagram carries the bit-depth idea; the before/after photo shows the payoff.
- **ingest-and-catalog** - optional: a screenshot-style contact sheet / grid of picks and rejects. Mostly diagram/workflow; photos optional.
- **order-of-operations** - optional before/after at a couple of stages. Diagram-first.
- **tone-and-the-curve** - a before/after of shadow-and-highlight recovery on a high-contrast frame (exploit X2D dynamic range). Pair with histograms if possible.
- **color-grading** - white-balance before/after (corrected vs cast), and a graded-for-mood version; optionally the same subject matched across Leica and Hasselblad color.
- **local-adjustments-and-masking** - a before/after where a masked local edit (dodged face, burned sky, subject separated) clearly changed where the eye goes.
- **black-and-white-conversion** - the same color frame converted several ways (neutral vs red-filter vs blue-filter mix) to show the channel choice; a split-toned version.
- **output-and-print** - optional: a screen-vs-print soft-proof comparison. Diagram-first.

## Part IV --- In practice

- **portrait-in-practice** - a full worked portrait (ideally the author's), with the leaf-shutter fill and finished skin tone; a before/after from raw to final.
- **street-and-documentary** - a few real street frames showing the decisive moment, zone focus keeping the scene sharp, and 28mm framing.
- **landscape-and-architecture** - a high-resolution landscape holding detail across the frame, and an architecture frame with verticals corrected (before/after keystone).
